Basics of Physics - Particle Physics

What is Particle Physics?

Particle Physics studies the fundamental particles that make up matter and radiation, and the interactions between them through fundamental forces.

Main thing to know:
Elementary particles, quantum fields, and fundamental forces are central concepts.

Key Equations in Particle Physics

\[ E^2 = (pc)^2 + (m c^2)^2 \]
Energy-momentum relation for a particle with mass \( m \) and momentum \( p \), where \( c \) is the speed of light.
\[ \mathcal{L} = \bar{\psi}(i \gamma^\mu D_\mu - m)\psi - \frac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} \]
Simplified form of the Lagrangian density in quantum field theory describing fermions \(\psi\) interacting with gauge fields \(F_{\mu\nu}\).
\[ \Gamma = \frac{1}{\tau} \]
Decay width \(\Gamma\) is the inverse of the particle's lifetime \(\tau\), characterizing unstable particles.
\[ \sigma = \frac{\text{Number of events}}{\text{Luminosity}} \]
Cross-section \(\sigma\) quantifies the probability of a particular interaction in a particle collision experiment.

Further Details

Elementary Particles and the Standard Model

Matter is composed of quarks and leptons, while interactions are mediated by gauge bosons like photons, W/Z bosons, and gluons, as described by the Standard Model.

Particle Interactions and Forces

The four fundamental forces—gravity, electromagnetism, weak, and strong nuclear forces—govern particle interactions and decay processes.

Applications of Particle Physics

  • Understanding the universe’s fundamental structure
  • Particle accelerators and collider experiments
  • Development of detectors and measurement techniques
  • Advancements in medical imaging and radiation therapy
