What is Electromagnetism?

Electromagnetism is the branch of physics that studies electric and magnetic fields and their interactions with charged particles. It unifies electricity, magnetism, and light as manifestations of the same fundamental force: the electromagnetic force. This force governs phenomena ranging from static electricity to electromagnetic waves, including visible light, radio waves, and X-rays.

Further Details

Electric and Magnetic Fields

Electric fields are generated by electric charges and exert forces on other charges. Magnetic fields arise from moving charges (currents) and affect moving charges or magnetic materials.

Electromagnetic Waves

Oscillating electric and magnetic fields can propagate through space as waves, carrying energy. Visible light, radio waves, microwaves, and X-rays are all examples of electromagnetic waves differing in frequency and wavelength.

Applications of Electromagnetism

  • Electric circuits and power generation
  • Communication technologies (radio, TV, cell phones)
  • Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
  • Electromagnetic radiation in astronomy and optics
