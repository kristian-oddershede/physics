What is Astrophysics?

Astrophysics is the branch of physics that studies the physical properties and behavior of celestial bodies and the universe as a whole. It applies principles from physics to understand stars, galaxies, black holes, and cosmology.

Main thing to know:
Gravity, light, and nuclear processes govern the cosmos.

Key Equations in Astrophysics

\[ F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \]
Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation: gravitational force between masses \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \) separated by distance \( r \).
\[ L = 4 \pi R^2 \sigma T^4 \]
Stefan-Boltzmann Law: total luminosity \( L \) of a star with radius \( R \) and surface temperature \( T \), where \( \sigma \) is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant.
\[ z = \frac{\lambda_{\text{observed}} - \lambda_{\text{emitted}}}{\lambda_{\text{emitted}}} \]
Redshift \( z \): measure of how much light from distant objects is stretched by the expansion of the universe.
\[ H_0 = \frac{\dot{a}}{a} \]
Hubble’s Constant \( H_0 \): rate of expansion of the universe, with \( a \) the scale factor.

Further Details

Gravitational Interactions

Gravity is the dominant force shaping the structure of stars, galaxies, and clusters of galaxies.

Stellar Processes

Stars generate energy through nuclear fusion in their cores, balancing gravitational collapse and emitting light and heat.

Cosmology

The study of the universe’s origin, evolution, and large-scale structure, including concepts like the Big Bang and dark matter.

Applications of Astrophysics

  • Understanding the life cycles of stars
  • Exploring black holes and neutron stars
  • Mapping cosmic microwave background radiation
  • Studying dark matter and dark energy
