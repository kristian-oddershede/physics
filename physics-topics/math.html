Basics of Physics - Mathematics

Why Mathematics Matters in Physics

Mathematics is the language of physics. To describe motion, forces, energy, and waves, physicists rely on mathematical tools such as trigonometry and calculus. A solid foundation in these areas is essential for understanding and solving problems in mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum physics, and beyond.

Main thing to know:
Trigonometry and Calculus are the backbone of introductory physics. They allow us to model oscillations, waves, fields, and rates of change.

Basic Derivatives

Derivatives measure rates of change. In physics, they describe velocity, acceleration, and how fields vary in space and time.

\[ \frac{d}{dx} (x^n) = n x^{n-1} \]
Power rule
\[ \frac{d}{dx} (\sin x) = \cos x, \quad \frac{d}{dx} (\cos x) = -\sin x \]
Derivatives of sine and cosine
\[ \frac{d}{dx} (e^{kx}) = k \cdot e^{kx} \]
Exponential function derivative
\[ \frac{d}{dx} (\ln x) = \frac{1}{x} \]
Natural logarithm derivative
\[ \frac{d}{dx} [f(x) g(x)] = f'(x) g(x) + f(x) g'(x) \]
Product rule
\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{f'(x) g(x) - f(x) g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \]
Quotient rule
\[ \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \]
Chain rule

Basic Integrals

Integrals measure accumulation. In physics, they describe displacement from velocity, work from force, and probability distributions.

\[ \int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1) \]
Power rule for integration
\[ \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C \]
Exponential integral
\[ \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \ln |x| + C \]
Logarithmic integral
\[ \int \sin x \, dx = -\cos x + C, \quad \int \cos x \, dx = \sin x + C \]
Trigonometric integrals

Applications in Physics

Oscillations and Waves

Trigonometric functions describe oscillatory motion such as springs and pendulums. Wave equations often involve sine and cosine functions, and their derivatives give velocity and acceleration of oscillations.

Motion and Kinematics

Derivatives connect position, velocity, and acceleration: \[ v(t) = \frac{dx}{dt}, \quad a(t) = \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} \]

Energy and Work

Integrals are used to calculate work done by a force: \[ W = \int F \cdot dx \]

Probability and Quantum Mechanics

Integrals are essential in quantum mechanics for normalizing wavefunctions and calculating probabilities.

Fourier Analysis Essentials

Fourier analysis is the study of representing functions or signals as sums of sinusoidal components. It is fundamental in physics for analyzing waves, vibrations, and quantum states. The key idea: any reasonably well-behaved function can be expressed as a combination of sines and cosines.

Fourier Series (Periodic Functions)

For a function \( f(x) \) with period \( 2\pi \), the Fourier series expansion is:

\[ f(x) = a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ a_n \cos(nx) + b_n \sin(nx) \right] \]
Decomposition of a periodic function into harmonics.

The coefficients are given by:

\[ a_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \, dx \] \[ a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \, dx \] \[ b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \, dx \]
Formulas for Fourier coefficients.

Fourier Transform (Non-Periodic Functions)

For functions defined on the whole real line, the Fourier transform generalizes the Fourier series:

\[ F(k) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) \, e^{-i k x} \, dx \]
Forward Fourier transform: converts a time/space function into frequency space.
\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} F(k) \, e^{i k x} \, dk \]
Inverse Fourier transform: reconstructs the original function from its frequency components.

Applications in Physics

  • Wave analysis: Decomposing complex vibrations into simple harmonics.
  • Quantum mechanics: Position and momentum wavefunctions are Fourier transforms of each other.
  • Signal processing: Filtering and analyzing frequency content of signals.
  • Optics: Diffraction and interference patterns are naturally described using Fourier transforms.
