What is Atomic & Optical Physics?

Atomic and Optical Physics explores the behavior and interaction of atoms, ions, and photons, focusing on their quantum properties and how light interacts with matter.

Main thing to know:
Energy levels, spectral lines, and light-matter interaction are central concepts.

Key Equations in Atomic & Optical Physics

\[ E_n = - \frac{13.6\, \text{eV}}{n^2} \]
Energy levels of the hydrogen atom (Bohr model), where \( n \) is the principal quantum number.
\[ E_{\text{photon}} = h \nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \]
Energy of a photon with frequency \( \nu \) or wavelength \( \lambda \), where \( h \) is Planck’s constant and \( c \) is the speed of light.
\[ \Delta E = h \nu = E_{\text{final}} - E_{\text{initial}} \]
Energy difference between atomic levels corresponds to absorption or emission of a photon.
\[ \vec{p} = \hbar \vec{k} \]
Momentum of a photon, with wavevector \( \vec{k} \) and reduced Planck’s constant \( \hbar \).

Further Details

Spectral Lines and Atomic Structure

Electrons in atoms occupy discrete energy levels. Transitions between these levels produce characteristic spectral lines used to identify elements.

Light-Matter Interaction

The absorption, emission, and scattering of light by atoms are fundamental processes in spectroscopy, lasers, and optical devices.

Applications of Atomic & Optical Physics

  • Laser technology
  • Atomic clocks and precision measurement
  • Quantum optics and quantum information
  • Spectroscopy for material analysis
