What is Quantum Mechanics?

Quantum Mechanics is the branch of physics that studies the behavior of matter and energy at the smallest scales — atoms, electrons, photons, and other fundamental particles. Unlike classical physics, quantum mechanics introduces concepts like wave-particle duality, quantization of energy, and inherent uncertainties.

Main thing to know:
The Schrödinger Equation governs the probabilistic evolution of quantum systems.

Key Equations in Quantum Mechanics

\[ i \hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) = \hat{H} \Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) \]
The Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation: describes how the quantum state \( \Psi \) evolves over time.

Here, \( \hbar \) is the reduced Planck constant, \( \hat{H} \) is the Hamiltonian operator representing the total energy, and \( \Psi \) is the wavefunction encoding the system's state.

\[ \hat{H} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 + V(\mathbf{r}) \]
Hamiltonian operator for a single particle with mass \( m \) in potential \( V(\mathbf{r}) \).
\[ \langle \hat{O} \rangle = \int \Psi^*(\mathbf{r}, t) \hat{O} \Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) \, d^3r \]
Expectation value of an observable \( \hat{O} \), calculated as the integral over the wavefunction.
\[ \Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} \]
Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle: position \( x \) and momentum \( p \) cannot both be precisely known.

Further Details

Wave-Particle Duality

Particles such as electrons exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties, depending on how they are measured.

Probabilistic Nature

Quantum mechanics provides probabilities of finding particles in certain states or positions, rather than definite outcomes.

Applications of Quantum Mechanics

  • Semiconductors and transistors (the basis of modern electronics)
  • Quantum computing
  • Laser technology
  • Atomic clocks and precision measurement
