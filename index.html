Basics of Physics

An introduction

This webpage can be used as a way to re-introduce key topics of physics as students start there Bachelor of Physics. It can also be used to give a formula overview, and short recap of key equations when looking back, after having followed a course and in exam preparation.

Key Methodologies in Physics

Physics relies on a combination of observation, experimentation, and mathematical modeling. This page focuses on key equations and nice models, to create an overview for students, to supplement the material given in their courses.

Key Trigonometric Identities

\[ \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \]
Pythagorean identity: fundamental relation between sine and cosine.
\[ \tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} \]
Definition of tangent in terms of sine and cosine.
\[ \sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \]
Double-angle formula for sine.
\[ \cos(2\theta) = \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \]
Double-angle formula for cosine.
