Basics of Physics

What is Physics?

Physics is the natural science that studies matter, its fundamental constituents, its motion and behavior through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force. It seeks to understand how the universe behaves at the most fundamental level.

Key Methodologies in Physics

Physics relies on a combination of observation, experimentation, and mathematical modeling. The scientific method is used extensively, involving hypothesis formulation, controlled experiments, data collection, and analysis to validate or refute theories.

Key Trigonometric Identities

\[ \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \]
Pythagorean identity: fundamental relation between sine and cosine.
\[ \tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} \]
Definition of tangent in terms of sine and cosine.
\[ \sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \]
Double-angle formula for sine.
\[ \cos(2\theta) = \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \]
Double-angle formula for cosine.
