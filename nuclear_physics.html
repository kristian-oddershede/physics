What is Nuclear Physics?

Nuclear Physics studies the components and behavior of atomic nuclei. It explains the forces and processes within the nucleus that govern nuclear reactions, stability, and radioactivity.

Main thing to know:
Mass-Energy Equivalence and the concept of nuclear binding energy are fundamental.

Key Equations in Nuclear Physics

\[ E = mc^2 \]
Einsteinâ€™s Mass-Energy Equivalence: mass \( m \) can be converted into energy \( E \), with \( c \) the speed of light.

This principle explains the huge energy released in nuclear reactions.

\[ B = \left( Z m_p + N m_n - m_{\text{nucleus}} \right) c^2 \]
Nuclear Binding Energy \( B \): energy required to disassemble a nucleus into protons (\( Z \)) and neutrons (\( N \)).
\[ \lambda = \frac{h}{p} \]
de Broglie wavelength \( \lambda \) of a particle with momentum \( p \), important in nuclear scattering.
\[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} \]
Radioactive decay law: \( N(t) \) is the number of nuclei at time \( t \), \( \lambda \) is the decay constant.

Further Details

Nuclear Forces and Stability

The strong nuclear force binds protons and neutrons tightly in the nucleus, overcoming electrostatic repulsion between protons.

Types of Nuclear Reactions

  • Fission: splitting of a heavy nucleus into lighter ones, releasing energy.
  • Fusion: combining light nuclei to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy.
  • Radioactive Decay: spontaneous emission of particles or radiation.

Applications of Nuclear Physics

  • Nuclear power generation
  • Medical imaging and radiation therapy
  • Understanding stellar processes
  • Nuclear weapons and safety
